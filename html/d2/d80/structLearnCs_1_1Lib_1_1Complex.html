Parses complex number from its string representation.

String representaition must be in form "(Re + Im)", e.g.:

Whitespace characters don't matter. The following forms are also legitimate:

◆ ParseRobust()

static Complex LearnCs.Lib.Complex.ParseRobust ( string  s)
inlinestatic

Parses complex number from its string representation.

String representation of various forms are allowed and whitespaces are ignored.

Basic representations (Re and Im are for real and imaginary values and can be replaced by any number representation understood by double.Parse(string); minimal spaces are included except where space contributes to readability):

"Re" - just the real part, no brackets around it (parsable by double.Parse(string)), e.g. " -2.3e6 "

"(Re)" - just the real part with brackets around it, e.g. " ( -2.3e6 ) "

"Im i" - just the imaginary part followed by "i", no brackets around it, e.g. "-2.3e6i", " -2.3e6 i ", "-2.3e6i "

"Im*i" - just the imaginary part followed by "*i", no brackets around it (parsable by double.Parse(string)), e.g. "-2.3e6 i"

Remark: cannot omit "i" when there is only the imaginary part.

"(Im i)" - just the imaginary part followed by "i" with brackets around it, e. g. " (-2.3e6i)", " (-2.3e6 i ) ", "(-2.3e6i ) "

Remark: cannot omit "i" when there is only the imaginary part.

"(Im*i)" - just the imaginary part followed by "*i", inside brackets, e. g. " (-2.3e6*i)", " ( -2.3e6 * i ) ", "( -2.3e6 *i) "

"Re, Im" - comma separated real and imaginary part, e.g. " -2.3e6, 7.48 ", "-2.3e6,7.48 "

"Re, Im i" - comma separated real part and imaginary part followed by "i", e.g. " -2.3e6, 7.48i ", "-2.3e6,7.48 i"

"Re, Im*i" - comma separated real part and imaginary part followed by "*i", e.g. " -2.3e6, 7.48*i ", "-2.3e6,7.48 * i"

"(Re, Im i)" - comma separated real and imaginary part, inside brackets, e.g. " ( -2.3e6, 7.48i) ", "(-2.3e6,7.48 i )"

"Re+Im i" - + separated real part and imaginary part followed by "i", e.g. " -2.3e6+ 7.48i ", " -2.3e6 + 7.48 i "

"Re+Im*i" - + separated real part and imaginary part followed by "*i", e.g. " -2.3e6+ 7.48*i ", " -2.3e6 +7.48 * i"

"Re-Im i" - - separated real part and imaginary part followed by "i", e.g. " -2.3e6- -7.48i ", " -2.3e6 --7.48 i ", " -2.3e6 - -7.48 i"

"Re-Im*i" - - separated real part and imaginary part followed by "*i", e.g. " -2.3e6- -7.48*i ", " -2.3e6 --7.48 *i ", " -2.3e6 - -7.48 * i"

"(Re+Im i)" - + separated real part and imaginary part followed by "i", inside brackets, e.g. " ( -2.3e6+ 7.48i ) ", "(-2.3e6 + 7.48 i)"

"(Re+Im*i)" - + separated real part and imaginary part followed by "*i", inside brackets, e.g. "(-2.3e6+7.48*i)", " ( -2.3e6 +7.48 * i )"

"(Re+Im i)" - - separated real part and imaginary part followed by "i", inside brackets, e.g. "( -2.3e6- -7.48i )", "(-2.3e6 --7.48 i)", " (-2.3e6 - -7.48 i)"

"(Re+Im*i)" - - separated real part and imaginary part followed by "*i", inside brackets, e.g. " ( -2.3e6- -7.48*i ) ", "(-2.3e6 --7.48 *i)", "( -2.3e6 - -7.48 * i)"

Practical examples of different representations:

Comma separated real and imaginary part, with or without round brackets, with or without 'i' for imaginary unit (optionally following *'):

"(Re, Im)": "(2.5 , 4.21)", "( 12.5,-1.22 )", "( 5.4,-2.5)", "( 3, 35 )", "(12e-5, -4.56e-10)"

"Re, Im ": "2.5 , 4.21", " 12.5,-1.22 ", " 5.4,-2.5", " 3, 35 ", "12e-5, -4.56e-10"

"(Re, Im i)": "(2.5 , 4.21 i)", "( 12.5,-1.22i )", "( 5.4,-2.5 * i)", "( 3, 35 *i )", "(12e-5, -4.56e-10*i)"

"Re, Im i": "2.5 , 4.21 i", " 12.5,-1.22 *i ", " 5.4,-2.5i", " 3, 35 * i", "12e-5, -4.56e-10*i"

Purely real numbers:

" ( 2.5 ) ", "(12.5)", "(5.4)", " ( 3.3 )", "(12.3e4)", "( 0.1e-6 )"

" 2.5 ", "12.5", "5.4", " 3.3 ", "12.3e4", " 0.1e-6 "

Purely imaginary numbers:

" ( 2.5 i ) ", "(12.5i)", "(5.4 * i)", " ( 3.3* i)", "(12.3e4*i)", "( 0.1e-6 * i)"

" 2.5 i ", "12.5i", "5.4" * i" 3.3* i", "12.3e4*i", " 0.1e-6 * i "
